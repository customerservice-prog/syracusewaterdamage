Why Syracuse Homeowners Call Us First

Syracuse averages over 127 inches of snowfall per year — the most of any major U.S. city. That means frozen pipes, ice dams tearing up rooflines, and heavy spring flooding as snow melts faster than drainage can handle. Older neighborhoods like Eastwood, Westcott, and Strathmore have aging infrastructure that makes water damage far worse when it hits.

We're locally based, we know Onondaga County's building codes and insurance carriers, and we know which Syracuse neighborhoods flood first when Onondaga Creek rises. That local knowledge means faster claims and fewer adjuster disputes for you.
